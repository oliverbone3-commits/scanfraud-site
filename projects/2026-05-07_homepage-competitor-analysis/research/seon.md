## Patterns to learn from
- Lifecycle-stage carousel (6 numbered steps) is a strong narrative spine — maps directly to how a fraud team thinks about coverage.
- Industries section binds each vertical to a named customer + a single bold metric — much more credible than abstract industry blurbs.
- Hover-reveal sub-capability tags on platform cards expose depth without cluttering the default view.
- Differentiator section explicitly names competitor categories ("data resellers & orchestrators") instead of vague "we're better" claims.
- Concrete inline numbers ("900+ signals", "14 days", "120+ R&D engineers", "up to 50%") embedded in prose, not just stat blocks.

## Patterns to skip
- Heavy reliance on the term "command center" — borderline buzzword, repeated in hero, eyebrow, and final CTA.
- Industries tab pattern hides 4 of 5 verticals behind a click — bad for SEO and skim-readers.
- Hero has no form, no demo video, no inline product visual that explains what the tool actually looks like.
- Six-step lifecycle carousel is animation-heavy and slow on mobile; users have to click through to read.
- No pricing, no integrations grid, no compliance/security badges visible on homepage — leaves enterprise buyer questions open.
