## Patterns to learn from
- Strong single-promise hero ("powers fearless commerce") paired with one CTA — no decision fatigue.
- Region-first framing (Europe / US / LATAM / Asia) is a sharp wedge for global ICPs and differentiates from generic "industries" grids.
- The guarantee/liability-shift pitch is the spine of the entire page — every product block ladders back to it.
- Mixes content/resource cards inline (Holiday Tracker, e-book) so the page doubles as a lead-gen surface, not just a brochure.
- Awards row uses real badges (G2, Payment Awards, BRC/MRC) — credible third-party proof, not vague "trusted by" copy.

## Patterns to skip
- Two stacked promo banners above the nav — visual noise, both dismissable but compete with hero.
- Resource cards appear duplicated mid-page and bottom-page — looks like a dynamic feed glitch and erodes polish.
- No numerical metrics anywhere in the hero or upper fold (no GMV, no merchants, no decision-speed) — relies entirely on the guarantee narrative.
- Hero CTA is just "Contact us" — soft, not action-coded ("see a demo", "calculate your savings", etc.).
- Heavy lifestyle photography, light on actual product UI — buyers can't see what the platform looks like.

## Notable copy patterns
- Eyebrow words used: ACCOUNT INTEGRITY, RISK RECOMMENDATIONS, RETURN ABUSE PREVENTION, GUARANTEED FRAUD PROTECTION, COMPLETE CHARGEBACK PROTECTION
- H1 length (words): 4
- CTA labels (verbatim): "Contact us", "See our customers", "VIEW THE DATA", "WATCH NOW", "Get the e-book", "Get the report", "Watch now", "Get a demo", "Login"
- Bullet count per feature: 3 (e.g., the three abuse types under Decision Center; three regional value props; three checkout-protection pillars)
