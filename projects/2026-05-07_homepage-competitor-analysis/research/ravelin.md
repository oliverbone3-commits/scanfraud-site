## Patterns to learn from
- Tabbed interfaces compress 6 use-cases and 5 differentiators into two compact sections instead of an endless scroll.
- Hero uses real product UI tiles (Prevent Rate, dispute rate chart, heatmap, network graph) as visual proof, not stock illustration.
- Compliance row gives each badge a sentence of context — turns a vanity bar into an actual trust signal.
- Quantified moats sit inside the differentiator copy ("9+ billion identity elements"), not in a separate metrics block.
- Case-study card carries the hard numbers (40.7%, 19.3%) — outcomes, not feature claims.

## Patterns to skip
- Light-grey on white in some places (low contrast on the outcome trio).
- Two near-identical CTAs ("Learn more" / "Book a call") — value-prop trio offers no demo path on hero.
- The "Strike the right balance" outcomes section is copy-only with no visuals or numbers — feels like filler.
- Vimeo embed in mid-page = extra third-party request and CLS risk.
- "Trusted by" logo strip with no logos visible in the extracted text — relies entirely on a carousel that may be slow to load.

## Notable copy patterns
- Eyebrow words used: TRUSTED BY, Just launched
- H1 length (words): 6 ("Customized protection for your unique fraud challenges.")
- CTA labels (verbatim): "Learn more", "Read our customers' stories", "Read our success stories", "Book a call", "Contact us", "Log in", "Download it here", "Watch now", "Read now"
- Bullet count per feature: 5 differentiator tabs, 6 use-case tabs, 4 outcome blocks; no inline bullet lists per feature — each tab has 2–4 sentence prose.
